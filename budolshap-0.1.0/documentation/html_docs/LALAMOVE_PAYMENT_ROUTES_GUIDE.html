💰 Lalamove Payment & Route Guide

Understanding Online Payment vs Cash on Delivery Scenarios

Budolshap E-Commerce Platform

📋 Overview

This guide explains how delivery routes and payment flows work differently depending on whether the customer pays online or chooses Cash on Delivery (COD). Understanding these scenarios is crucial for implementing the correct Lalamove API configuration.

🎯 Key Concept: The "Route" in Lalamove refers to the physical path the driver takes, which changes based on the payment method. Online payments have simple routes (Seller → Buyer), while COD can require round trips.

Scenario 2A: Cash on Delivery - "Abono" Method

Driver pays seller first, then collects from buyer
COD COMMON
⚠️ Important: This method is typically used for items worth less than ₱2,000. The driver acts as a temporary bank, using their own money to pay the seller, then getting reimbursed by the buyer.

📍 The Route

🏪 Point A
Seller's Store
→
🏠 Point B
Buyer's Address
⚠️ One-way trip, but driver handles cash at both stops

🔄 Step-by-Step Flow

1
📦 Pickup at Seller's Store (Point A)
  • Driver arrives at the seller's location
  • Driver shows Order ID to seller
  • Seller tells driver the item cost (e.g., ₱500)
  • Driver pays the seller ₱500 from their own pocket
  • Seller hands over the package
  • Driver confirms pickup in Lalamove app
💵 Money Exchange: Driver pays Seller ₱500 (item cost)
2
🚗 Transit to Buyer
Driver navigates to the buyer's delivery address. Driver is now ₱500 out of pocket.
3
🏠 Delivery to Buyer (Point B)
  • Driver arrives at buyer's location
  • Buyer pays driver the TOTAL amount (₱500 item + ₱65 delivery = ₱565)
  • Driver hands over the package to buyer
  • Driver marks order as "Completed"
  • Driver keeps the ₱565 (reimbursing themselves the ₱500 + earning ₱65 delivery fee)
💵 Money Exchange: Buyer pays Driver ₱565 (₱500 item + ₱65 delivery)

💰 Cost Breakdown

Product Cost: ₱500.00
Delivery Fee: ₱65.00
Driver Collects from Buyer: ₱565.00

💸 Money Flow Diagram

At Pickup (Point A):
👤 Driver → ₱500 → 🏪 Seller
Driver is now -₱500 out of pocket
At Delivery (Point B):
🏠 Buyer → ₱565 → 👤 Driver
Driver gets ₱500 back + ₱65 delivery fee = ₱565 ✓

✅ Advantages

  • Still one-way trip (cheaper than round trip)
  • Faster than round trip method
  • Buyer can inspect item before paying
  • No need for buyer to have online payment
  • Seller gets paid immediately

❌ Disadvantages

  • Driver must have cash on hand
  • Limited to lower-value items (usually < ₱2,000)
  • Risk if buyer refuses to pay
  • Driver bears temporary financial risk
  • Cash handling can cause delays

Scenario 2B: Cash on Delivery - Round Trip Remittance

Driver collects from buyer and returns cash to seller
EXPENSIVE HIGH VALUE
⚠️ Important: This method is used for high-value items (> ₱2,000) or when the seller doesn't trust the driver to pay upfront. The driver must make a round trip back to the seller to remit the cash.

📍 The Route

🏪 Point A
Seller's Store
→
🏠 Point B
Buyer's Address
→
🏪 Point A
Back to Seller
⚠️ Round trip required - Delivery fee is approximately DOUBLE

🔄 Step-by-Step Flow

1
📦 Pickup at Seller's Store (Point A - First Visit)
  • Driver arrives at the seller's location
  • Driver shows Order ID to seller
  • Seller hands over the package
  • Seller tells driver: "Collect ₱5,000 from buyer and bring it back to me"
  • Driver confirms pickup in Lalamove app
💵 Money Exchange: NONE - No payment at pickup
2
🚗 Transit to Buyer
Driver navigates to the buyer's delivery address with the package.
3
🏠 Delivery to Buyer (Point B)
  • Driver arrives at buyer's location
  • Buyer pays driver ₱5,000 (item cost only)
  • Driver hands over the package to buyer
  • Driver now has ₱5,000 cash that belongs to the seller
💵 Money Exchange: Buyer pays Driver ₱5,000 (item cost)
4
🚗 Return Trip to Seller
Driver drives back to the seller's store with the cash. This is why it's called a "round trip."
5
🏪 Cash Remittance at Seller's Store (Point A - Second Visit)
  • Driver arrives back at seller's location
  • Driver hands over ₱5,000 cash to seller
  • Seller verifies the amount
  • Driver marks order as "Completed"
  • Order is finished ✓
💵 Money Exchange: Driver gives Seller ₱5,000 (collected from buyer)

💰 Cost Breakdown

Product Cost: ₱5,000.00
Delivery Fee (Round Trip): ₱130.00 (approximately 2x normal fee)
Buyer Pays: ₱5,000.00 (to driver at delivery)
Platform Charges Seller: ₱130.00 (delivery fee)

✅ Advantages

  • Suitable for high-value items
  • Driver doesn't need large cash reserves
  • Seller gets cash directly from driver
  • Lower risk for driver (not using own money)
  • Buyer can inspect item before paying

❌ Disadvantages

  • Expensive: Delivery fee is approximately double
  • Slow: Takes much longer (round trip)
  • Seller must wait for driver to return
  • More fuel consumption and emissions
  • Driver spends more time on single order
  • Not efficient for the platform

💡 Implementation Recommendations for Budolshap

✅ Recommended Approach

  1. Primary Method: Online Payment
    • Encourage customers to pay online via PayMongo/GCash
    • Offer small discounts for online payment (e.g., free delivery over ₱500)
    • Simplest, fastest, and cheapest option
  2. Secondary Method: COD - Abono (for items < ₱2,000)
    • Enable for customers who prefer cash payment
    • Set maximum item value limit (e.g., ₱1,500)
    • Clearly communicate that buyer pays driver at delivery
  3. Avoid: COD - Round Trip
    • Only use for very high-value items if absolutely necessary
    • Inform customer of higher delivery fee upfront
    • Consider alternative: Require online payment for items > ₱2,000

🔧 Technical Implementation

✅ Best Practices

For Online Payment Orders:

  • ✓ Verify payment is confirmed before booking Lalamove
  • ✓ Include "PREPAID - NO COLLECTION" in delivery remarks
  • ✓ Send confirmation to both buyer and seller
  • ✓ Provide tracking link immediately after booking

For COD Orders:

  • ⚠️ Set maximum item value limits (e.g., ₱1,500 or ₱2,000)
  • ⚠️ Clearly display total amount buyer must pay
  • ⚠️ Include exact amount in delivery remarks for driver
  • ⚠️ Send SMS reminder to buyer before delivery
  • ⚠️ Have contingency plan if buyer doesn't have exact change
  • ⚠️ Consider adding small COD processing fee (₱10-20)

Things to Avoid:

  • ❌ Don't use round trip for low-value items (waste of money)
  • ❌ Don't allow COD for very high-value items without verification
  • ❌ Don't forget to include delivery fee in COD total
  • ❌ Don't assume drivers always have change
  • ❌ Don't book delivery before payment confirmation (online orders)

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What happens if the buyer doesn't have exact change for COD?
A: This is a common issue. Best practices:
  • Send SMS reminder with exact amount needed
  • Drivers usually carry some change, but not guaranteed
  • Consider rounding to nearest ₱10 or ₱20
  • Have customer support ready to handle disputes
Q: Can I mix payment methods (partial online, partial COD)?
A: Technically possible but NOT recommended. It creates confusion for drivers and customers. Choose one method per order.
Q: What if the driver doesn't have enough cash for Abono method?
A: This can happen if the item cost is high. Solutions:
  • Set item value limits for COD (e.g., max ₱1,500)
  • Lalamove drivers are usually prepared for amounts up to ₱2,000
  • For higher amounts, require online payment instead
  • Driver may cancel if they can't pay - have backup plan
Q: How do I know which method Lalamove used for my COD order?
A: Check the number of stops in the order:
  • 2 stops (A → B): Abono method
  • 3 stops (A → B → A): Round trip method
  • You specify this when creating the order via API
Q: Should I charge customers more for COD?
A: Yes, it's common practice:
  • Add ₱10-20 COD processing fee
  • Covers risk and complexity of cash handling
  • Incentivizes online payment (which is better for you)
  • Clearly display this fee during checkout
