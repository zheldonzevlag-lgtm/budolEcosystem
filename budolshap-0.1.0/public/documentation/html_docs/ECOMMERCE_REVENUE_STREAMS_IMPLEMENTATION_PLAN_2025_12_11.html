1. Advertising & Marketing Services High Revenue Potential

This is typically the second largest revenue stream after commissions. It allows sellers to pay for visibility.

How to Attain

  • Sponsored Search: Allow sellers to bid on keywords so their products appear first in search results.
  • Homepage Banners: Sell premium "hero" slots on the main page.
  • Flash Sale Slots: Charge sellers a fee to participate in high-traffic flash sale events.
  • Push Notifications: Offer paid blasts to user segments.

Implementation Plan

Phase 1: Basic "Boost" Functionality (MVP)

Create a simple system where sellers can pay a flat fee to "boost" a product for a set duration (e.g., 24 hours).

  • Add is_boosted and boost_expiry fields to Product schema.
  • Update search/ranking algorithm to prioritize boosted items.
  • Create a payment flow for sellers to purchase boosts using their wallet/credits.
Phase 2: Slot Management & Banners

Develop an admin interface to sell specific slots.

  • Create a "Campaigns" module where admins define slots (Homepage, Category Top).
  • Build a seller interface to apply for these slots.
  • Automate the scheduling: when a slot is paid for, it automatically goes live at the start time.
Phase 3: Bidding System (Advanced)

Implement a CPC (Cost Per Click) model.

  • Tech Stack Upgrade: Likely requires a specialized engine (e.g., Elasticsearch) to handle real-time ad serving and budget tracking.
  • Features: Keyword bidding, day-parting, and budget caps.
  • Reporting: Detailed analytics dashboard for sellers (Impressions, Clicks, ROAS).

2. Logistics & Fulfillment Services Operational Revenue

Moving beyond just facilitating delivery, the platform can take a cut or charge for premium handling.

How to Attain

  • Shipping Spread: Negotiate bulk rates with couriers (like Lalamove/J&T) and charge users the standard rate, keeping the difference.
  • Fulfillment by Platform: Offer warehousing where you pick, pack, and ship for sellers for a fee.
  • Insurance: Offer shipping insurance at checkout.

Implementation Plan

Phase 1: Shipping Markup & Insurance

Simplest to implement immediately.

  • Logic: In your calculateShipping() helper, add a configurable margin or handling fee.
  • Feature: add a checkbox at checkout "Add Shipping Protection for ₱X" that is pure revenue (minus claims).
Phase 2: Aggregated Logistics

Integrate multiple providers but centralized billing.

  • Sellers drop off packages at your hub, or you do a "milk run" collection.
  • You sort and hand over to couriers.
  • Requires physical infrastructure or partnerships with 3PLs.
Phase 3: Warehousing (Fulfillment)

Full service storage and picking.

  • System: Need a WMS (Warehouse Management System).
  • Integration: When an order comes in, it splits into "Merchant Fulfilled" and "Platform Fulfilled" automatically.

3. Financial Services (Fintech) Sticky Revenue

Financial products increase platform stickiness and provide high-margin revenue.

How to Attain

  • Seller Lending: Offer loans based on their sales history (low risk since you control the cash flow).
  • Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL): Partner with or build a BNPL service for buyers.
  • Withdrawal Fees: Small fee for instant cashouts.

Implementation Plan

Phase 1: Withdrawal & Transaction Fees

Monetize the movement of money.

  • Implement a "Wallet" system (which you are seeing in Phase 3 of Escrow).
  • Add a small fixed fee or % for withdrawals to bank accounts.
  • Add a fee for "Instant" withdrawals vs "Standard" (1-3 days).
Phase 2: Seller Cash Advance

Use data to offer credit.

  • Algorithm: Analyze last 3 months of sales to determine credit limit.
  • Mechanism: Disburse loan to Seller Wallet, deduct daily repayment from incoming sales automatically.

4. Subscription Programs Recurring Revenue

Predictable monthly income from power users.

How to Attain

  • Seller Pro: remove ads, get lower commission rates, advanced analytics.
  • Buyer VIP: Free shipping vouchers, exclusive deals.

Implementation Plan

Phase 1: Seller Tiers

Define two tiers: Standard and Premium.

  • Database: Add subscription_tier and subscription_expires_at to Store model.
  • Logic: Wrap advanced features (like bulk upload, export CSV, advanced analytics) in a check for this tier.
  • Billing: Recurring billing integration (PayMongo subscriptions).

5. Live Commerce & Engagement Trend

Monetizing attention.

How to Attain

  • Live Selling Commission: Take a higher cut from live-stream sales due to the provided bandwidth/platform.
  • Virtual Gifts: Viewers buy virtual stickers to send to streamers.

Implementation Plan

Phase 1: Integration

Integrate a streaming provider (like Mux or specialized SDKs like Agora).

  • Allow sellers to "Go Live".
  • Pin products to the stream.
Phase 2: Gamification

Add "Coins" or "Diamonds" users can buy and tip sellers with.

  • Platform takes ~30-50% cut of tips.

6. Data Monetization Enterprise Revenue

Selling insights rather than products. Large brands will pay to understand consumer behavior on your platform.

How to Attain

  • Brand Reports: detailed analytics on how their brand performs vs competitors (anonymized).
  • Trend Forecasting: sell data on what categories are rising to manufacturers.

Implementation Plan

Phase 1: Internal Analytics

Build robust tracking of views, cart additions, and conversions per category/attribute.

Phase 2: Seller Dashboard "Pro" Stats

Included in the subscription (see above), giving sellers access to their own funnel data.

Phase 3: External Data products

Package aggregated data (e.g., "Cosmetics trends in Q4 2025") and sell as PDF reports or API access to large consulting firms.

7. Value-Added Services (VAS) Service Revenue

Acting as an agency for your sellers.

How to Attain

  • Photography Services: Sellers send you products, you take pro photos.
  • Store Setup/Design: Charge a fee to design their store layout (if you have custom pages).
  • Customer Service Outsourcing: Sellers pay you to handle their chat support.

Implementation Plan

Phase 1: Service Catalog

Create a "Services" marketplace within the Seller Center where they can book these services.

Phase 2: Partner Network

You don't need to do it yourself. Onboard freelance photographers or agencies and take a commission (20%) on every booking made through your platform.

8. White-Label & Ecosystem Expansion

Monetizing the tech stack itself.

How to Attain

  • SaaS Solution: Allow brands to use your engine to power their own dot-com sites (like Shopify).
  • Cross-Selling: Sell mobile load, bill payments, or travel tickets within the app.

Implementation Plan

Phase 1: Digital Goods Integration

Integrate APIs for Mobile Load (e.g., Reloadly) and Bill Payments. These are high-frequency, low-margin transactions that keep users opening the app.
