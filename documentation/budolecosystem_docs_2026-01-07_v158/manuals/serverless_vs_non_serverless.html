Serverless vs. Non-Serverless Comparison v158

A strategic analysis of infrastructure choices for the budolEcosystem, focusing on performance, scalability, and financial feasibility.

Serverless (Vercel / AWS Lambda)

  • Pros:
    • Zero maintenance of OS or runtime.
    • Automatic horizontal scaling.
    • Free Tier friendly (Hobby plans).
  • Cons:
    • Cold starts (2-5s) impact user experience.
    • No native persistent Sockets (requires workarounds).
    • Execution time limits (10-30s).

Non-Serverless (VPS / EC2 / Docker)

  • Pros:
    • Native Socket.io support (always connected).
    • Zero cold starts (Always "warm").
    • Full control over the environment.
  • Cons:
    • Manual OS patching and security.
    • Scaling requires manual intervention or complex ALB setups.
    • Fixed monthly cost even if idle.

Why Non-Serverless Wins for budolEcosystem

For a fintech application like budolEcosystem, the primary drivers are Real-time updates (via Sockets) and Predictable performance. Serverless architectures on free tiers often struggle with:

  1. Socket Disconnections: Vercel functions are ephemeral and terminate after 10 seconds, making native Sockets impossible.
  2. Transaction Latency: Cold starts during a payment process can lead to timeouts and inconsistent data states.

Final Recommendation: The Hybrid Path

We recommend a Hybrid Infrastructure for the best balance of cost and performance:

  • Frontend (Vercel): Keep the Next.js/React frontend on Vercel for global CDN delivery and easy deployments.
  • Backend (Docker on VPS/EC2): Move the 11 microservices to a persistent server. This allows the api-gateway to maintain stable Socket.io connections for the whole ecosystem.
  • Database (Managed AWS RDS): Use a managed database for data integrity and automated backups.

Result: $10-$15/month total cost with professional-grade performance and stable real-time features.
